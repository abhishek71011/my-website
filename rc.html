This is an image-processing demonstration, not a diagnostic device. Vessel detection and artery/vein classification use color, morphology, and tree-connectivity heuristics computed in your browser — even published deep-learning models trained on thousands of expert-labeled images top out around 85–95% agreement on this task, and expert human graders disagree with each other too, so treat any single classification here as an estimate, not ground truth. Diameters are estimated from a standard optic-disc calibration, not a measured camera scale.
Pipeline

What happens to your image

Four passes, all running locally on your device — nothing is uploaded to a server.

01 — Enhance

Vessel filtering

The green channel is isolated and run through a multi-scale ridge filter that responds to thin, elongated structures — the vessels — while suppressing the disc, fovea, and noise.

02 — Segment

Adaptive thresholding

The enhanced response is locally thresholded against its neighborhood to produce a binary vessel mask that holds up across uneven illumination.

03 — Reduce

Skeletonization

A Zhang–Suen thinning pass erodes the mask to a one-pixel-wide centerline tree, preserving connectivity and branch points.

04 — Classify

Artery / vein split

Each vessel branch is scored on local hue, saturation, and the presence of a bright central reflex — arteries run narrower with that pale streak, veins run wider and darker — then a topology pass checks that ambiguous branches agree with the confidently-classified vessels they connect to, mirroring how arteries and veins form two separate trees from the optic disc.
